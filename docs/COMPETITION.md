## Verdict: threat HIGH (revised up from MEDIUM-HIGH)

Captiq already claiming Nepali means the moat is **not "Nepali at all"** — it narrows to
**Ninglish-native + Devanagari typography depth + the correction-loop data flywheel**.
CapCut (free) sets the pricing floor: a paid product must be clearly better at Nepali or
it dies to free.

**Strategy:** win the Ninglish/tech beachhead fast; build the correction-loop data moat
early (Phase 2, month 2) — the one advantage incumbents can't copy without your Nepali
correction dataset.

**Must validate in interviews:** is kalakar/Captiq's Nepali actually good enough already?
(If yes, the wedge is thinner than assumed.)
